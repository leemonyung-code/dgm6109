Visualization: Study Time vs. Delayed Recall

Hypothesis & Question

Question: How does different study method and the duration of a study time affect my performance to recall vocabs 24 hours later?

Hypothesis: I hypothesize that there will be a positive correlation between study time and delayed recall score. However, I also predict that active study methods (like Mnemonic Imagery) will get higher retention rates than passive methods (like Massed Practice), regardless of the time spent.

Data Overview

The data presented here was collected over a period of 4 weeks (October 1st to October 28th, 2025). Each data point represents a daily study session.

  • Collection Frequency: Once per day.
  • Key Variables:
    • Study Time: Duration of the session in minutes (X-axis).
    • Delayed Recall Score: A test score out of 15 points, taken 24 hours after studying (Y-axis).
    • Method: The technique used, represented by color.
    • Self-Rated Confidence: A score (7-8.5) representing how confident I felt, represented by circle size (larger circles = higher confidence).
  • Completeness: The dataset includes 28 consecutive days of records, satisfying the requirement for 4+ weeks of data.

Conclusion

Based on the visualization, there appears to be a general positive correlation between study time and delayed recall score. However, the method of study plays a significant role. "Massed Practice" data points (orange) tend to cluster lower on the Y-axis. Additionally, larger circles (higher confidence) often correlate with higher Recall Scores, suggesting that my self-assessment of learning quality is generally accurate. "Mnemonic Imagery" (red) shows both high scores and larger circle sizes, indicating high effectiveness and confidence.
