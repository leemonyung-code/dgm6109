Plot 1: Confidence vs. Delayed Recall by Method and Time

Hypothesis: As my self-rated confidence (0-10) increases, my delayed recall score increases.
In addition: Study time (circle size) and study method (color) are shown to explore other potential correlations.

Reason Why This is One of My Best Scatterplots

This visualization effectively displays four different data properties (confidence, recall score, study time, and method) in a single, clear chart. It directly tests my primary hypothesis by plotting confidence on the X-axis and recall on the Y-axis.

By using color for 'method' and circle size for 'studyTime', it allows for a deeper analysis of secondary factors. For example, we can look to see if one study method (a specific color) clusters in the high-confidence, high-recall area.

Technically, this plot also correctly uses Array.sort() to draw the largest circles first. This prevents smaller data points from being hidden behind larger ones and significantly improves the chart's legibility.
